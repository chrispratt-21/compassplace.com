Privacy Policy

Effective Date: July 3, 2026 • Last Updated: July 24, 2026

1. Introduction

First Call Advantage ("FCA," "we," "us," or "our") is a service operated by Fletcher Street Corporation, a Delaware corporation. FCA provides lead response and intake advocacy services to home care agencies ("Agency Partners" or "Agencies").

This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information in connection with the FCA platform, website, mobile application, and related services (collectively, the "Services"). It applies to:

  • Agency Partners — home care agencies and their authorized personnel who access the FCA platform.
  • Advocates — individuals employed or contracted by FCA to respond to incoming leads on behalf of Agency Partners.
  • Lead Contacts — individuals whose contact and care information is received by FCA via referral from Agency Partners and their marketplace partners.
  • Website Visitors — anyone who visits FCA's web properties.

By using the Services, you acknowledge this Privacy Policy. If you are an Agency Partner, your use of FCA is also governed by your Business Associate and Services Agreement (BASA) with Fletcher Street Corporation, which serves as both the governing services agreement and, where applicable, a Business Associate Agreement under HIPAA.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information About Agency Partners and Their Personnel

When an Agency Partner enrolls in FCA, we collect:

  • Agency name, address, and business contact information
  • Names, email addresses, and phone numbers of agency personnel who access the platform
  • Login credentials (passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text)
  • Billing and payment information (processed by our payment processors; we do not store full card numbers)
  • Communication preferences and platform usage data

2.2 Lead Contact Information

When an Agency Partner's marketplace lead is forwarded to FCA, we receive and process:

  • Name of the individual seeking care or their family decision-maker
  • Phone number and email address
  • General information about care needs, including type of care sought, urgency, and relevant care context
  • The raw email message from which this information is extracted
  • Call history and outcome notes from advocate interactions

Lead contacts have not directly interacted with FCA. Their information is provided to us by Agency Partners who received it from third-party marketplace platforms. Agency Partners are responsible for ensuring that their sharing of this information with FCA is authorized under the terms of their agreements with those marketplaces and under applicable law.

2.3 Information About Advocates

For FCA advocates, we collect:

  • Full name and phone number (used for authentication and lead notification)
  • Device push notification tokens (to deliver real-time lead alerts)
  • On-duty status and availability history

2.4 Automatically Collected Information

When you use the FCA web console or mobile application, we may automatically collect:

  • Device type, operating system, and app version
  • IP address and general location
  • Usage patterns, session duration, and feature interactions
  • Error logs and diagnostic data

5. Health Information and Sensitive Data

Important Notice Regarding Health Information Information about an individual's care needs (such as medical conditions, diagnoses, or functional limitations) may constitute sensitive personal information and, in some contexts, Protected Health Information (PHI) under HIPAA.

FCA operates as a service provider to home care agencies. Some agencies may qualify as Covered Entities or Business Associates under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA); others may not, depending on the nature of the care they provide.

If your agency is subject to HIPAA and intends to share PHI with FCA, this sharing is governed by Part Two of your Business Associate and Services Agreement (BASA) with Fletcher Street Corporation, which serves as a Business Associate Agreement under 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e). Do not activate lead forwarding if your agency is a HIPAA Covered Entity until your BASA has been executed. Contact us at connect@compassplace.com with any questions.

We treat care-related information with heightened care regardless of HIPAA applicability. Access is restricted to authorized advocates and agency personnel. We do not use care information for any purpose other than providing the FCA service to the originating agency.

Care context notes are retained as part of the lead record for the duration of your agency's relationship with FCA and for a reasonable period thereafter for audit purposes (see Section 7).

6. Legal Bases for Processing

For users in jurisdictions where a legal basis for data processing is required, FCA relies on the following:

  • Contractual necessity — processing necessary to perform our service agreement with Agency Partners.
  • Legitimate interests — operating, improving, and securing the Services; communicating with users about their accounts.
  • Legal obligation — complying with applicable law, court orders, or regulatory requirements.
  • Consent — where we have obtained your consent (such as push notification permission from Advocates).

7. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide the Services and as required by law. General retention guidelines:

  • Agency and user account data — retained for the duration of the agency's active relationship with FCA, plus up to 3 years thereafter for legal and audit purposes.
  • Lead records and care notes — retained for the duration of the agency relationship, plus up to 2 years thereafter. Agencies may request earlier deletion by contacting us.
  • Raw email messages — retained for up to 90 days after processing, then deleted unless a lead record is associated with the message.
  • Advocate device tokens — soft-deleted on sign-out; purged from the database after 90 days of inactivity.
  • Server logs — retained for up to 90 days.

PHI retained by FCA upon termination of a BASA is subject to the return-or-destroy obligations set forth in Section 15.9 of the BASA.

8. Security

We implement technical and organizational measures to protect personal information, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher) and at rest
  • Role-based access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel
  • Multi-factor authentication options for platform accounts
  • Hashed password storage using industry-standard algorithms
  • Regular review of access privileges

No system is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we are committed to notifying affected parties promptly in the event of a breach that involves your personal information, as required by applicable law. For HIPAA Covered Entities, breach notification is governed by Section 15.7 of your BASA.

9. Lead Contacts: Your Rights and Options

If your information was shared with FCA as part of a marketplace lead referral and you would like to:

  • Request a copy of the information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information from our systems
  • Opt out of further contact through FCA

Please contact us at connect@compassplace.com. We will respond within 30 days. Please note that some information may need to be retained for legal or audit purposes even after a deletion request.

10. Account Deletion

If you are an Agency Partner user or an Advocate with a platform account, you have the right to request deletion of your account and associated personal information at any time.

To delete your account, you have two options:

  • In the app: Navigate to your account settings in the FCA mobile application and select "Delete Account." Your account and associated data will be removed from our active systems promptly.
  • By email: Send a deletion request to connect@compassplace.com with the subject line "Account Deletion Request." Include the name and email address associated with your account. We will process your request and confirm deletion within 30 days.

Please note that some information may be retained after deletion where required by law or for legitimate audit purposes, as described in Section 7 (Data Retention). Lead records associated with your agency's activity may also be retained per our agreement with the originating Agency Partner.

11. California Residents

If you are a California resident, you may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to delete your personal information, the right to correct inaccurate information, and the right to opt out of the sale of personal information.

FCA does not sell personal information. To exercise your rights under California law, contact us at connect@compassplace.com.

12. Children's Privacy

FCA is a business-to-business service not intended for individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a minor, contact us immediately at connect@compassplace.com.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. Material changes will be communicated to Agency Partners via email. Continued use of the Services after the effective date of any change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
